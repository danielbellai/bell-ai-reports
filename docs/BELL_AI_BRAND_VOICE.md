# Bell AI Solutions Brand Voice Skill

This skill ensures every piece of content produced for Bell AI Solutions is on-brand, consistent, and sounds like it came from a real person who builds automation systems for a living.

## Before You Write Anything

Read these rules. Then write. Then run the brand check at the bottom.

## Brand Identity

**Company:** Bell AI Solutions
**Founder:** Daniel Shih
**What we do:** Build productized automation systems for local businesses at fixed prices with monthly retainers.
**Who we serve:** Local business owners and operators (real estate brokerages, accounting firms, cleaning agencies, nonprofits) running teams of 3 to 50 people.
**Anchor line:** "We build systems that run without you."

## Voice: Approachable Expert

We're friendly but authoritative. We know more than the client about automation and we're not shy about that. But we never talk down. We explain in plain English. We focus on what changes for the client, not on how the tech works under the hood.

### Voice Attributes

**Direct:** Say what you mean. No filler sentences. Get to the value fast.
**Knowledgeable:** Show expertise through specifics, not jargon. Name real tools and real outcomes.
**Conversational:** Write like you're talking to the client over coffee. Contractions. Short sentences. Real talk.
**Outcome-focused:** Always connect what we build to what the client gets. Hours saved. Leads closed. Revenue gained.
**Confident:** No hedging. No "might" or "potentially." We stand behind our work because we've done it before.

## Writing Rules (Mandatory)

These rules apply to every piece of content. No exceptions.

1. **Use contractions.** "You'll" not "you will." "We're" not "we are." Stiff writing kills trust.
2. **Lead with outcomes, not features.** The client cares about what changes for them. Not the tech stack.
3. **Use specifics.** "10 hours a week" beats "significant time savings." "90-second response" beats "fast follow-up." Real numbers build credibility.
4. **No jargon without context.** If you mention a tool name, explain what it does in the same sentence. If you say "webhook," translate it to "automatic trigger."
5. **One idea per paragraph.** If you're packing two points into one block of text, split them.
6. **Vary sentence length.** One short sentence. Then a longer one that builds the point. Then another short one. Don't write at a uniform pace.
7. **Start sentences imperfectly sometimes.** "And," "But," "So," "Here's the thing." At least 15% of sentences should start with a conjunction or conversational fragment.
8. **Take a stance.** Every section should contain at least one direct opinion. "This is wrong." "Ignore anyone who tells you otherwise." "This works better than anything else we've tested."

## Banned Words and Phrases

These are instant AI detection signals. Never use them in Bell AI content.

**Banned verbs:** leverage, harness, delve, embark, navigate (as metaphor), foster, showcase, bolster, underscore, spearhead, elevate, illuminate, facilitate, endeavor, transcend, empower

**Banned adjectives:** seamless, groundbreaking, transformative, compelling, meticulous, robust, comprehensive (as filler), unparalleled, invaluable, paramount, pivotal, crucial, vibrant

**Banned nouns:** tapestry, landscape (as metaphor), realm, beacon, paradigm, synergy, interplay, testament, toolkit (as metaphor), roadmap (as metaphor)

**Banned phrases:** "In today's fast-paced world," "ever-evolving," "it's not just X, it's Y," "from X to Y" (faking range), "in an era where," "let's dive in," "unlock the potential," "at the end of the day," "the question isn't X, it's Y," "This is where X comes in"

**Banned punctuation:** Em dashes (the number one AI writing tell). Use periods, commas, colons, or parentheses instead. Semicolons (too formal, too academic). Split into two sentences.

## Brand Check (Run After Every Draft)

Before delivering any content, run through this:

- [ ] Does it sound like a person wrote it? Read it aloud.
- [ ] Is the core promise ("systems that run without you") present or implied?
- [ ] Are we leading with outcomes, not features?
- [ ] Are there specific numbers or results? Vague claims erode trust.
- [ ] Zero banned words from the list above?
- [ ] Zero em dashes? Zero semicolons?
- [ ] Would a busy local business owner understand every word?
- [ ] Does the tone match "approachable expert"? Not too casual, not too corporate?
- [ ] Contractions used throughout?
- [ ] Sentence length varies? No metronome pacing?
